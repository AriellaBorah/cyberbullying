There are many consequences of cyberbullying


The victims will often experience symptoms such as:

Depression

Anxiety

Changes in eating and sleeping patterns

Decreased academic achievements and attendance

Loss of interest in activities they used to enjoy


Less evident signs of cyberbullying may include:

An increase or decrease in the usage of electronic devices

Shutting down social media accounts

Showing negative emotional responses when using their devices.


Victims also have a higher rate of suicide and may resort to extremely violent measures such as school shootings.
Cyberbullying will often impact their social interactions and affect their ability to form meaningful, lasting, relationships.
In addition to the mental effects, the victims may also experience physiological effects.
They might have frequent headaches and stomach pain that are often associated with nervousness.
Other long term effects may include: low self esteem, chronic fatigue and insomnia, lower performances at school or work, and higher rates of substance abuse.
