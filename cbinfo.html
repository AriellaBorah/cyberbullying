What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of technology to harass, threaten, embarrass or target another person.

Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, and/or sharing negative, harmful, or false information about someone using online platforms such as social media, forums, text messages, instant messages, and any other places where users can share and view content.

This type of bullying can often leave permanent results if the information and posts are not taken down.


There are apparently four types of cyberbullies. They include:

The Vengeful Angel: Consists of people aiming to protect victims of cyberbullying.

The Power Hungry: As the name suggests, they want attention and power over others

The Inadvertent Cyberbully: Consists of victims of bullying that want to get revenge on their tormentors.

The Mean Girls: Often consists of a group with a predetermined target and do this to gain status or approval. They don't actually have to be girls


There are also multiple methods used by cyberbullies. These include:

Harassment: The use of repeated offensive messages to target a victim.

Exclusion: The practice of isolating a person online.

Outing: The sharing of a victim's personal information to online platforms.

Masquerading: The use of a fake online identity to harass and degrade thier victims.
